## Summary

US Federal Banking Regulations covering **Third Party** risk. This note covers 4 regulations with their objectives, bank size applicability, and role responsibilities.

## Regulations

### OCC Bulletin 2013-29 Third-Party Relationships
**Objective:** Require banks to assess and manage risks from third-party service providers.

**Applies to:** National banks; principles adopted by other regulators

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### OCC Bulletin 2023-17: Interagency Guidance on Third-Party Relationships: Risk Management
**Objective:** Require banks to assess and manage risks from third-party service providers.

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### FDIC FIL-44-2008 Third-Party Risk Guidance
**Objective:** Outline risk management expectations for outsourcing and vendor relationships.

**Applies to:** FDIC-insured banks

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### FFIEC TPRM
**Objective:** final guidance (PDF)on managing risks associated with third-party relationships. The guidance provides sound principles that support a risk-based approach to third-party risk management that banking organizations may consider when developing and implementing risk management practices for all stages in the life cycle of third-party relationships.

**Applies to:** Banks under 100 Billion in Assets

**Roles:** Board, CRO, CTO, CISO
