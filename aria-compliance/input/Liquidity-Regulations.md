## Summary

US Federal Banking Regulations covering **Liquidity** risk. This note covers 10 regulations with their objectives, bank size applicability, and role responsibilities.

## Regulations

### Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) Rule (12 CFR 50, 249, 329)
**Objective:** Ensure banks maintain enough high-quality liquid assets to survive short-term liquidity stress.

**Applies to:** Banks with assets > $250 billion or > $10 billion in foreign exposures

**Roles:** CRO

### Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)
**Objective:** Promote stable long-term funding structure to reduce liquidity risk.

**Applies to:** Banks with assets > $250 billion

**Roles:** CRO

### Interagency Guidance on Funding and Liquidity Risk Management
**Objective:** Set expectations for effective liquidity risk management programs.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO

### Dodd-Frank Act Stress Testing (DFAST, 12 USC 5365; 12 CFR Part 252)
**Objective:** Stress Testing

**Applies to:** Any US bank holding company with average total consolidated assets of $100 billion or more;

**Roles:** CRO

### Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review (CCAR)
**Objective:** the Federal Reserve's annual assessment of large U.S. bank holding companies (BHCs) to ensure they have enough capital to withstand severe economic stress, requiring firms to submit forward-looking capital plans that detail their stress testing, capital adequacy, and planned capital distributions (dividends, buybacks) under various scenarios

**Applies to:** applies to large U.S. bank holding companies (BHCs) with over $100 billion in assets, subjecting firms over $250 billion

**Roles:** CRO

### SR 15-18: Federal Reserve Supervisory Guidance on Stress Testing
**Roles:** CRO

### Basel III Capital Rules (12 CFR Parts 3, 217, 324)
**Objective:** U.S. banking regulations implementing the international Basel III standards for capital adequacy, requiring banks to hold sufficient high-quality capital (Common Equity Tier 1, Tier 1, Total Capital) against their risk-weighted assets (RWAs) and leverage, with specific rules for different bank sizes

**Applies to:** Large Banks (G-SIBs/Large Regional)

**Roles:** CRO

### Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR, 12 CFR Parts 50, 249, 329)
**Objective:** mandates that large banks hold enough High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA) to cover projected net cash outflows for a 30-day stress period, requiring a minimum ratio of 1.0 (or 100%), ensuring short-term resilience against financial shocks by holding easily sellable assets for potential deposit withdrawals and obligations

**Applies to:** large, internationally active banks and bank holding companies (BHCs) with over $250 billion in assets or $10 billion in

**Roles:** CRO

### SR 10-6: Interagency Policy Statement on Funding and Liquidity Risk Management
**Objective:** guidance articulates the process that depository institutions should follow in appropriately identifying, measuring, monitoring, and controlling their funding and liquidity risks. In particular, the guidance re-emphasizes the importance of cash flow projections, diversified funding sources, stress testing, a cushion of liquid assets, and a formal, well-developed contingency funding plan as primary tools for measuring and managing funding and liquidity risks.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO

### SR 12-7: Guidance on Stress Testing for Banking Organizations with More Than $10 Billion in Total Consolidated Assets
**Objective:** Guidance related to how a banking organization should develop and implement its stress testing framework in a manner commensurate with its size, complexity, business activities, and overall risk profile.

**Applies to:** Banks over 10Bn in assets

**Roles:** CRO
