## The pitfall

Common questions and answers on **Risk Review & Revision** in banking risk management, drawn from expert practitioner knowledge.

## Why it matters

Getting Risk Review & Revision wrong is a frequent examination finding. These Q&As capture the practitioner perspective on what regulators expect and where banks fall short.

## How to avoid it

### How should a bank monitor it's risk performance?

A Bank should look to have processes in place to assess business unit and organizational performance against key things like risk appetite metrics. Bank should be actively evaluateing that everything is working as intended and within pollicy, limits etc. Assessment of performance may need to result in recalibration of metrics, thresholds and limits as needed.

**Key considerations:** Ensure your ERM policy addresses the process and cadence for risk review and revision. For example, establish that Performance reporting to key management and Board committees should take place no less than quarterly and that the Board and C Suite should evaluate output of performance results as an input into annual strategic planning actitivies.
