## The pitfall

Common questions and answers on **Communication & Reporting** in banking risk management, drawn from expert practitioner knowledge.

## Why it matters

Getting Communication & Reporting wrong is a frequent examination finding. These Q&As capture the practitioner perspective on what regulators expect and where banks fall short.

## How to avoid it

### How should a bank ensure a culture of risk awareness and be able to evidence this to regulators?

A communication & training plan should be a key component of your ERM. Regulators place an emphasis on developing a risk aware culture within a bank that has a shared understanding of risk management and roles and responsibilities in managing risk across the 3 lines of defense.

**Key considerations:** WIthin your ERM Policy, ensure there is a section that addresses expectations related to communication and training. Start by developing an annual communication plan that is used to define what information will be shared, the audience it will be shared with, how it will be shared and the frequency for sharing it (i.e. Board Risk Topic training, quarterly, in person presentation etc.). Also ensure that you include  mandatory annual training is in place for all employees related to specific risk topics such as BSA/AML, ERM, Operational Risk, Cyber Risk etc. Training to Board members on key risk topics should be provided quarterly. An internal intranet page dedicated to risk management should be in place
