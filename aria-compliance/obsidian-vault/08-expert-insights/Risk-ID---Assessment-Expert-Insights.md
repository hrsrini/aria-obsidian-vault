## The pitfall

Common questions and answers on **Risk ID & Assessment ** in banking risk management, drawn from expert practitioner knowledge.

## Why it matters

Getting Risk ID & Assessment  wrong is a frequent examination finding. These Q&As capture the practitioner perspective on what regulators expect and where banks fall short.

## How to avoid it

### Why do banks need risk identification and assessment programs in place?

Banks should have standardized, repeatable risk identification, assessment & measurement programs in place as part of its ERM. These types of programs should drive risk prioritization (e.g. methodology in place to determine risk weightings etc.). All risks and controls should be captured in an inventory and aligned to an overarching Risk Taxonomy for eash of classification and reporting

**Key considerations:** Start by developing and implementing key risk ID & assessment programs across the bank (e.g. Operational Risk Event & RCSA programs). Ensure your Operational Risk Event Program that has documented criteria defined related to what constitutes an operational risk event for that bank, thresholds that will trigger things like investigation, root cause analysis and reporting processes. Build out and implement a Risk & Control Self-assessment Program across the bank that looks key risks and mitigating controls agains key processes within the bank, documents potential high risk areas, control deficiencies as well as responses/action plans to address control gaps
