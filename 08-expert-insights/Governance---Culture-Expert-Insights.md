## The pitfall

Common questions and answers on **Governance & Culture** in banking risk management, drawn from expert practitioner knowledge.

## Why it matters

Getting Governance & Culture wrong is a frequent examination finding. These Q&As capture the practitioner perspective on what regulators expect and where banks fall short.

## How to avoid it

### Why do I need a committee structure in place?

Established Governance Structure demonstrates a strong tone from the top. This addresses regulatory expectation RE: Transparency around reporting lineage (e.g. info flowing from your committees up to the board)

**Key considerations:** Create a governance policy that outlines criteria for committees. Ensures there are controls/processes in place around committee criteria. Not everything should be a committee, somethings are better served as a working group under a committee which is expected to have less rigour (i.e. no charter, less formal, may not required minutes etc.)

### What should a Risk Organization Design Consider?

Key considerations include a number of things based on the size and complexity of your bank. For example, as your bank grows in size you will need to have Resources in place with specialized skills needed for areas such as ERM, MRM, TPRM, Cyber across your 3 lines of defense to manage risk effectively. What you currently have in place related to risk management and what regulators require you to have in place for the size of your bank all factor into the bredth and maturity of the org design you need to have established.

**Key considerations:** Conduct a current state analysis of your risk management program and benchmark where you are as a bank agains regulatory requirements for Financial & Non Financial risk categories for you bank's asset size. Prioritize gaps you need to address based on regulatory expectation. Create a 12-36 month roadmap outlining your timelines for implementing solutions to resolve your self identified program gaps

### When we first pass $10Bn in assets, what are the key risk programs I need to have in place?

While regulators will look for all of your Non-financial and Financial risk programs to be built to support your asset size as you grow, key programs they will expect to have in place quickly will be programs such as ERM, ORM, MRM, TPRM, Cyber & BCP as well as expansion of your BSA/AML program. You will also be expected to enhance reporting/governance to facilitate deeper transparency related to risks & controls/key issues to your Board.

**Key considerations:** Ensure you have an enterprise risk management framework in place across the bank that outlines how you will id, assess, measure, monitor and control your risks across the organization. For risk programs, ensure that stand up of new risk programs such as ORM, MRM etc, have been captured in your roadmap and have milestones defined related to timing of implementation of key components such as Policies, staffing, procedures, development and implementation of assessments, metrics etc. Ensure that all risk programs link to expectations set out in your ERM framework and policy. When in doubt, consider networking with peer banks to get a sense of the bredth and depth of program maturity they have in place for their programs as regulators may benchmark your programs agains the maturity of their programs,
