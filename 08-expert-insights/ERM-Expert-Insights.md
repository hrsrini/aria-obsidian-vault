### What is the differency between a Policy & Framework?

A Framework outlines how risk management will be done within a bank while a policy sets out specifics around what must be done to comply with the framework

**Key considerations:** ERM Framework: look to include statements around how risk will be managed across the 3 lines of defense. Include statements related to tools and methodologies to be used to manage risk across the bank etc. ERM Policy: Aligned to your ERM Framework, set requirements and expectations related to what must be done to ensure compliance with the framework, define things like Risk Appetite, goals, roles and responsibilities across the 3 lines of defnese as well as Governance. Address strategies for risk mitigation and controls.

### Why is reporting a focus for regulators?

Banks are required to provide reporting to the Board related to key issues and risks in an effort to drive real-time awareness and transparency. As a bank grows in size and complexity, the expectations around the bredth and depth of information reported to the Board as well as the frequency increases.

**Key considerations:** Within your Franework and policies, ensure that you include sections related to how your bank will monitor and report on issues and risks on an on-going basis. Adress items such as who will see the reports, what they should include, (i.e. risk exposure, risk appetite breaches, mitigation plans etc.) and the frequency requirements for reporting (e.g. monthly, quarterly, annually etc.). Ensure reporting includes information related to emerging risks arising from new products, technology or regulatory changes alon with plans for how the bank will address these changes to mitigate risk.
