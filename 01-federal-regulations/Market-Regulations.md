## Summary

US Federal Banking Regulations covering **Market** risk. This note covers 2 regulations with their objectives, bank size applicability, and role responsibilities.

## Regulations

### Market Risk Capital Rule (12 CFR 3 Subpart F, 12 CFR 217 Subpart F)
**Objective:** Require banks to maintain capital against market risk exposures.

**Applies to:** Large banks with > $1 billion in trading assets/liabilities

**Roles:** CRO

### Volcker Rule (Section 619, Dodd-Frank Act; 12 CFR 44, 248, 351, 255)
**Objective:** Limit proprietary trading and ownership of hedge/private equity funds to reduce market risk.

**Applies to:** Banks with assets > $10 billion

**Roles:** CRO
