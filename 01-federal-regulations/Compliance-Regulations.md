**Objective:** Prevent money laundering and terrorist financing through transaction monitoring and reporting.

### Bank Secrecy Act/Anti-Money Laundering (BSA/AML, 31 USC 5311 et seq.; 31 CFR Chapter X)
**Objective:** The BSA authorizes the Department of the Treasury to impose reporting and other requirements on financial institutions and other businesses to help detect and prevent money laundering. Specifically, the regulations implementing the BSA require financial institutions to, among other things, keep records of cash purchases of negotiable instruments, file reports of cash transactions exceeding $10,000 (daily aggregate amount), and to report suspicious activity that might signify money laundering, tax evasion, or other criminal activities. ### Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) Regulations (31 CFR Parts 500-599)
**Objective:** U.S. Treasury's rules for enforcing economic and trade sanctions, found under Title 31, Chapter V of the Code of Federal Regulations, covering prohibitions on transactions with sanctioned countries, entities, and individuals, including licensing, reporting, and penalties for violations, essential for U.S. persons globally to block assets and halt prohibited dealings to protect national security.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO

### USA PATRIOT Act (Pub. L. 107-56; 31 USC 5318(i))
**Objective:** Requires financial institutions to implement Customer Identification Programs (CIPs) and report suspicious activities, aiming to track terrorist financing. Key aspects include enabling broader data sharing, increasing penalties for terrorism-related crimes, and adding anti-terrorism measures to the BSA.

**Applies to:** All Banks

**Roles:** CRO

### Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA, 15 USC 1681 et seq.)
**Objective:** U.S. law regulating consumer reporting agencies (CRAs) to ensure the accuracy, fairness, and privacy of personal information in consumer reports (credit reports). It grants consumers rights to access their reports, dispute inaccuracies, and limits who can access their data, promoting responsible collection and use of information for credit, employment, insurance, and other purposes.

**Applies to:** All Banks

**Roles:** CRO

### Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA, 12 USC 2801; Regulation
**Objective:** U.S. federal law requiring financial institutions to report data on mortgage applications and originations, including borrower demographics and loan terms, to help the public and regulators monitor for discriminatory lending patterns and ensure fair access to housing credit

**Roles:** CRO

### Military Lending Act (MLA, 10 USC 987; 32 CFR Part 232)
**Objective:** The Military Lending Act (MLA) is a federal law protecting active-duty service members, reservists on active duty, and their spouses/dependents from predatory lending by capping the Military Annual Percentage Rate (MAPR) at 36% on many credit products, including payday loans, auto title loans, and credit cards, and banning mandatory arbitration and other unfair practices.

**Applies to:** All Banks

**Roles:** CRO

### Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA, 50 USC 3901 et seq.)
**Objective:** The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA), codified at 50 U.S.C. Â§ 3901 et seq., protects active-duty U.S. military members by temporarily suspending certain civil actions and transactions that could harm their legal rights or financial well-being during service, allowing them to focus on national defense. **Roles:** CRO

### NACHA Operating Rules (National Automated Clearing House Association)
**Objective:** foundational rules for the Automated Clearing House (ACH) Network, defining roles for financial institutions and participants to ensure smooth electronic payments, with recent updates focusing heavily on fraud prevention, requiring all originators (businesses) to implement risk-based processes to detect and stop fraudulent transactions by mid-2026, alongside ongoing compliance for security, data handling, and account validation.

**Roles:** CRO

### Regulation CC (Availability of Funds and Collection of Checks, 12 CFR Part 229)
**Objective:** mandates how quickly banks must make deposited funds available to customers, implementing the Expedited Funds Availability Act (EFAA) and Check Clearing for the 21st Century Act (Check 21) to speed up check processing and provide faster access to money. It sets rules for fund availability schedules, requires disclosure of these policies, and establishes procedures for handling "substitute checks" (Check 21), ensuring more efficient check clearing and consumer protection.

**Roles:** CRO
