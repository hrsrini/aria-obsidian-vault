## Summary

US Federal Banking Regulations covering **Technology/InfoSecurity/Data** risk. This note covers 11 regulations with their objectives, bank size applicability, and role responsibilities.

## Regulations

### FFIEC IT Examination Handbook & AIO Booklet
**Objective:** Set standards for IT governance, operations, and controls in financial institutions.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### Banking Agency Interagency Guidance on Information Security
**Objective:** mandates that financial institutions implement comprehensive written programs with administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect customer data confidentiality, integrity, and availability, requiring risk assessments, board oversight, and secure third-party management, all rooted in laws like the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) to prevent fraud and unauthorized access, ensuring proper disposal of information.

**Applies to:** All Banks

**Roles:** Board, CRO, CTO, CISO

### Computer-Security Incident Notification Rule (12 CFR Parts 53, 225, 304)
**Objective:** mandates that U.S. banking organizations and their service providers report significant cyber incidents, known as "notification incidents," to their primary federal regulators within 36 hours of detection, with specific rules for service providers notifying banks of disruptions lasting over four hours. Issued by the FDIC, OCC, and Federal Reserve

**Applies to:** All Banks

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### FFIEC Business Continuity Planning Booklet
**Objective:** provides guidance and examination procedures to assist examiners in
evaluating financial institution and service provider risk management processes to ensure
the availability of critical financial services.

**Applies to:** All Banks

**Roles:** Board, CRO, CTO, CISO

### BCBS 239
**Objective:** establishing strong frameworks for risk data aggregation and reporting, focusing on clear ownership, accountability, data quality (accuracy, completeness, timeliness), robust IT infrastructure, and data lineage, all to enable better risk management and decision-making, especially during crises, by ensuring data is reliable and traceable from source to report, as mandated by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS).

**Applies to:** Large Banks - Specifically: G-SIBs: Global Systemically Important Banks were the primary focus for mandatory compliance.

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) Safeguards Rule
**Objective:** Require banks to develop, implement, and maintain information security programs to protect customer data.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### GLBA Privacy Rule
**Objective:** Mandates privacy notices to customers, controls sharing of nonpublic personal information, and provides consumers with opt-out rights regarding data sharing.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
**Objective:** Grants California residents rights over their personal data, including access, deletion, and opt-out from sale; requires banks to ensure consumer data transparency and protection.

**Applies to:** Banks doing business in California; regardless of asset size

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### Right to Financial Privacy Act (RFPA)
**Objective:** Protects confidentiality of consumer financial records by limiting government access and requiring customer consent for disclosures.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### Interagency Guidelines for Information Security (GLBA)
**Objective:** Establish standards for protecting the security and confidentiality of customer information.

**Applies to:** All banks

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO

### NYDFS Cybersecurity Regulation (23 NYCRR 500)
**Objective:** Mandate comprehensive cybersecurity programs for financial institutions chartered in New York.

**Applies to:** Banks operating in New York; applies regardless of asset size

**Roles:** CRO, CTO, CISO
